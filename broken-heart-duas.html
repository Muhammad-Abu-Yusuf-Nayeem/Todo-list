لَا مَلْجَأَ وَلَا مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلَّا إِلَيْكَ
La malja'a wa la manja minka illa ilayk
"There is no refuge and no escape from You — except to You"

These are the du'as of the broken heart — دعاء الانكسار — the most beloved supplications to Allah, spoken by those who have nowhere left to go

read
DU'A ONE — THE BEGINNING
لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ
La hawla wa la quwwata illa billah
"There is no power and no strength — except through Allah"
Bukhari & Muslim
I cannot move. I cannot breathe. I cannot exist — without Him. Every muscle, every heartbeat is His gift. The Prophet ﷺ called this a treasure from the treasures of Paradise.
DU'A TWO — FROM THE DARKEST PLACE
لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنْتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ
La ilaha illa anta subhanaka inni kuntu minaz-zalimin
"There is no god but You — Glory be to You — Indeed I have wronged myself"
Quran 21:87 — Du'a of Prophet Yunus
Said from inside the whale — in complete darkness — with no escape. Three layers: Tawheed, glorification, admission of guilt. Allah answered immediately. The most powerful du'a of total admission ever recorded.
DU'A THREE — THE CRY OF NEED
رَبِّ إِنِّي لِمَا أَنْزَلْتَ إِلَيَّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ فَقِيرٌ
Rabbi inni lima anzalta ilayya min khayrin faqir
"My Lord — I am in absolute need of whatever good You send down to me"
Quran 28:24 — Du'a of Prophet Musa
Prophet Musa said this alone, hungry, exhausted, with nothing. He did not specify what he wanted. He simply said — I need You. Just three words of need — and Allah changed his entire life that same day.
DU'A FOUR — THE WHISPER OF PAIN
رَبِّ إِنِّي مَسَّنِيَ الضُّرُّ وَأَنْتَ أَرْحَمُ الرَّاحِمِينَ
Rabbi inni massaniyad-durru wa anta arhamur-rahimin
"My Lord — suffering has touched me — and You are the Most Merciful of all"
Quran 21:83 — Du'a of Prophet Ayyub
After years of illness and total loss. He did not demand. He did not complain. He simply said — I am hurting — and You are Merciful. No demands. No conditions. Just truth placed before Allah. That was enough.
DU'A FIVE — THE MOST ASTONISHING
أَعُوذُ بِرِضَاكَ مِنْ سَخَطِكَ، وَبِمُعَافَاتِكَ مِنْ عُقُوبَتِكَ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْكَ
A'udhu biridaka min sakhatik, wa bimu'afatika min 'uqubatik, wa a'udhu bika mink
"I seek refuge in Your pleasure from Your anger — in Your pardon from Your punishment — and I seek refuge in You — from You"
Muslim — Du'a of Prophet ﷺ in Sujood
The most astonishing sentence in Arabic literature — أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْكَ — seeking refuge in Allah's mercy from Allah's justice. Only Allah can be both the thing you fear — and the only shelter from that fear.
DU'A SIX — WHEN EVERYTHING IS GONE
حَسْبِيَ اللَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ عَلَيْهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَهُوَ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ
Hasbiyallahu la ilaha illa huwa 'alayhi tawakkaltu wa huwa rabbul 'arshil 'azim
"Allah is enough for me — there is no god but Him — upon Him I rely — He is the Lord of the Mighty Throne"
Quran 9:129
When everything is gone — wealth, health, people, hope — say this. It means: I have lost everything — but I still have Allah — and Allah is everything. The Prophet ﷺ said whoever says this seven times morning and evening, Allah will take care of all his concerns.
DU'A SEVEN — THE SOUL RETURNING HOME
إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ
Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un
"Indeed we belong to Allah — and indeed to Him we shall return"
Quran 2:156
Not just for death. For every loss. Every door that closes. Every heartbreak. It means — I came from Him. Everything I love came from Him. It all returns to Him. I am at peace. This is surrender in its most beautiful form.
DU'A EIGHT — THE MASTER OF FORGIVENESS
اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي
Allahumma anta rabbi la ilaha illa anta, khalaqtani wa ana 'abduk, abu'u laka bini'matika 'alayya wa abu'u bidhanbī faghfir li
"O Allah You are my Lord — no god but You — You created me and I am Your slave — I acknowledge Your blessings upon me — and I acknowledge my sin — so forgive me"
Bukhari — Sayyidul Istighfar
The complete confession. No hiding. No excuses. Just — here I am. All of me. Sinful and Yours. The Prophet ﷺ said whoever says this with conviction in the morning and dies that day enters Paradise.
DU'A NINE — THE FINAL SURRENDER
أَسْلَمْتُ نَفْسِي إِلَيْكَ، وَفَوَّضْتُ أَمْرِي إِلَيْكَ، وَأَلْجَأْتُ ظَهْرِي إِلَيْكَ، رَغْبَةً وَرَهْبَةً إِلَيْكَ، لَا مَلْجَأَ وَلَا مَنْجَا مِنْكَ إِلَّا إِلَيْكَ
Aslamtu nafsi ilayk, wa fawwadtu amri ilayk, wa alja'tu zahri ilayk, raghbatan wa rahbatan ilayk, la malja'a wa la manja minka illa ilayk
"I have submitted my soul to You — I have entrusted my affairs to You — I have leaned my back against You — in hope and in fear — there is no refuge and no escape from You — except to You"
Bukhari — Du'a before sleep, Prophet ﷺ
Every word is a layer of surrender. My soul — submitted. My affairs — entrusted. My back — leaning on You. My hope — in You. My fear — of You. And then it ends where it always ends — nowhere to go. Except You.
